About Me

I am an emerging researcher with a strong passion for exploring science through both computational and experimental approaches. My work spans bioinformatics and computational biology, including microarray data analysis, RNA-seq and scRNA-seq studies, and genome-wide investigations. I have expertise in computational chemistry, insecticide design, computer-aided drug design, cheminformatics, homology modeling, and molecular dynamics simulations. I also apply machine learning and deep learning to enhance predictive accuracy in biological and chemical research.

In addition, my work in phytochemistry includes evaluating cytotoxicity, dose–mortality relationships, repellency, larvicidal, piscicidal, and molluscicidal activities. By integrating these diverse skills, I aim to develop innovative solutions and contribute meaningful insights at the intersection of biology, chemistry, and computational science.
